# Attention Spanner — demo video storyboard

Target length: ~2:00–2:30 (core scenes only run ~90s if you need a shorter cut —
those are marked OPTIONAL below and can be dropped).

Prep before recording:
- Seed the new-tab dashboard with real-looking data: a couple of gym check-ins,
  an in-progress article and video, a few tasks, a non-zero streak, some XP.
  An empty dashboard demos poorly.
- Close unrelated tabs/bookmarks bar. Use a clean browser profile if possible.
- Turn on "do not disturb" so no OS notifications pop mid-recording.
- Decide narration style: voiceover while recording, or silent + captions
  added in editing. Script below works for either.

---

## Scene 1 — Hook (0:00–0:10)
**Screen:** Talking head or a blank new tab.
**Action:** None yet — this is the problem statement.
**Say:** "I used to open ten articles, read none of them, and lose every task
I meant to write down the second I thought of it. So I built a Chrome
extension that fixes that."

---

## Scene 2 — Quick capture (0:10–0:20)
**Screen:** Any webpage, not the extension.
**Action:** Press `⌘⇧Y`. A small capture window pops up. Type a task
("Reply to Sarah's email"), hit Enter. Window closes instantly.
**Say:** "Anywhere in Chrome, one shortcut captures a task before it
disappears from my head."

---

## Scene 3 — RSS reading + resume (0:20–0:40)
**Screen:** Click the toolbar icon → Feeds tab in the popup. Open an article.
Scroll halfway down, then switch to a different tab (simulate getting
distracted).
**Action:** Show the tab-switch nudge notification appearing.
**Say:** "It tracks how far I get into an article. If I wander off mid-read,
it nudges me back — not annoyingly, just enough."

---

## Scene 4 — Continue reading (0:40–0:50)
**Screen:** Open the popup again → Continue Reading pane, or the dashboard's
Continue card.
**Action:** Click an in-progress article; it resumes at the scroll position.
**Say:** "And it always knows exactly where I left off."

---

## Scene 5 — New-tab dashboard (0:50–1:10)
**Screen:** Open a new tab.
**Action:** Slow pan across the dashboard: activity calendar at top, then
Gym / Brain dump / Tasks / Focus cards, then Continue / Links / Progress /
Flashcards below.
**Say:** "Every habit lives on one dashboard: reading, tasks, gym, focus
sessions, flashcards — plus a GitHub-style activity graph of the whole year."

---

## Scene 6 — AI brain dump (1:10–1:30)
**Screen:** Dashboard's Brain dump card.
**Action:** Type a messy stream-of-consciousness note (e.g. "need to email
prof about extension, also buy protein, and finish the react bug from
yesterday"). Click **Structure**. Show it turning into clean bullets +
proposed tasks. Confirm one into the task list.
**Say:** "Chrome's built-in Gemini Nano model runs entirely on-device to turn
a messy brain dump into structured tasks — no API key, nothing leaves my
machine."

---

## Scene 7 — Gamification (1:30–1:50)
**Screen:** Dashboard Progress card + Gym card.
**Action:** Click the gym "I went today" check-in button. Show XP tick up,
level bar move, a quest line complete, and (if you have one queued) a badge
unlock notification.
**Say:** "Reading, focus time, gym check-ins, and flashcards all feed one XP
pool — levels, badges, and weekly quests, so consistency actually feels
rewarding."

---

## Scene 8 — Focus Mode (1:50–2:10)
**Screen:** Popup's Focus tab or dashboard Focus card.
**Action:** Click a 25-minute focus block. Try to open a blocked site (e.g.
a social media tab) — show the blocked page with the countdown and open
tasks. Optionally show the 5-second hold-to-quit control.
**Say:** "Focus Mode blocks distracting sites at the browser level, so it
survives closing the tab or even restarting Chrome."

---

## Scene 9 (OPTIONAL) — Flashcards (2:10–2:20)
**Screen:** Flashcards tab.
**Action:** Review a couple of cards, show the SM-2 rating buttons (Again/
Hard/Good/Easy).
**Say:** "It even generates spaced-repetition flashcards from your notes."

---

## Scene 10 — Close (2:20–2:30)
**Screen:** Back to the dashboard, or a title card with the GitHub link.
**Action:** None, or slow zoom out.
**Say:** "It's all open source. Link's in the post below — I'd love your
feedback."

---

## Editing notes
- Cut Scene 3's nudge notification tight — dead air while waiting for it to
  fire will drag. Consider pre-triggering it and cutting in the moment it
  appears.
- Scene 7 is the most "wow" moment (XP + badge popping) — don't rush it.
- If trimming to ~90s: keep Scenes 1, 2, 5, 6, 7, 10. Cut 3, 4, 8, 9.
